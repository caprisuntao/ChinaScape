# Interview Records 

## Interview Responses

### Interview 1
**Q: What is your primary goal when visiting a new city in China?**  
A: I want to understand why a place is famous. I want to know the history behind it in English.

**Q: What is the biggest hurdle you face?**  
A: Navigation. I struggle to explain to taxi drivers where I need to go.

**Q: What feature would make your life easier?**  
A: A Culture Guide that explains local customs and a way to show addresses in Chinese characters to locals.

**Q: What do you want from ChinaScape?**  
A: Clear Before You Go checklists (ID requirements, booking links).

**Q: How do you currently find information about Chinese attractions?**  
  A: I use travel blogs, WeChat groups, and Google searches; info is scattered and sometimes in Chinese only.

**Q: What features would help you most?**  
  A: English descriptions, suggested itineraries,ticket booking links, and cultural notes.

**Q: What worries you about visiting attractions in China?**  
  B: Safety, transport reliability, and not understanding local customs.

**Q: Would you use itinerary planning tools?**  
  B: Yes, if they are simple, mobile-friendly, and show costs.

## Key Insights from Interviews

Based on the interview responses, several key themes emerge regarding the needs and challenges of international travelers visiting China:

1. **Cultural Understanding and Historical Context**: Travelers prioritize learning the "why" behind attractions, seeking English-language historical explanations rather than just visual experiences. This suggests a need for in-depth, educational content.

2. **Language Barriers and Navigation**: A major hurdle is dealing with Chinese-only apps, communication with locals, and navigation. Features like address translation to Chinese characters and culture guides are highly desired.

3. **Planning and Booking Challenges**: Last-minute planning is common, but issues arise with advance bookings (e.g., WeChat mini-programs), ID requirements, and scattered information sources. Clear checklists and booking links are essential.

4. **Information Sources and Accessibility**: Current reliance on blogs, groups, and searches leads to fragmented info. Preferred features include English descriptions, offline maps, and mobile-friendly itinerary tools with costs and times.

5. **Itinerary Preferences**: Day-by-day plans, costs, are favored, indicating a need for practical, flexible planning tools.
